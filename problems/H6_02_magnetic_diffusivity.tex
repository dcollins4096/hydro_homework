
   \pts{4} For $10^4$K plasma, what's the largest structure with a decay
    time shorter than the age of the universe?

\solution{
  \emph{Solution}

  From (14.13), $\lambda = 10^7 \rm{cm}^2 \rm{s}^{-1}$ for a $10^4$K plasma.
  Thus $L = \sqrt{\tau \lambda} = (4.3 \times 10^{17} s * 10^7)^{1/2} \approx
  10^{12} \rm{cm}\approx 0.1 \rm{AU}$.  So fields of larger structures take
  longer than the age of the universe to decay.
}
